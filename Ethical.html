Ethical Mandate

Argument

If water pollution is destroying the lives of people who do not cause it, then an injustice is being committed against them.

Large companies cause water pollution by dumping their waste into water supplies making it undrinkable without being affected

by it. This is an injustice against the people who rely on that water.

"And I brought you into a plentiful land to enjoy its fruits and its good things. But when you came in, you defiled

my land and made my heritage an abomination." -Jeremiah 2:7

UN Articles

Article 25

(1) Everyone has the right to a standard of living adequate for the health and well-being of himself and of his family, including food,

clothing, housing and medical care and necessary social services, and the right to security in the event of unemployment, sickness,

disability, widowhood, old age or other lack of livelihood in circumstances beyond his control. (2) Motherhood and childhood are entitled

to special care and assistance. All children, whether born in or out of wedlock, shall enjoy the same social protection.


Water is the most essential component of life and the well being and health of a person or family cannot be guaranteed without water.

The availability of clean water is out of the average person's control because they rely on the environment and larger companies and

agencies to provide it for them.


Article 2

Everyone is entitled to all the rights and freedoms set forth in this Declaration, without distinction of any kind, such as race,

colour, sex, language, religion, political or other opinion, national or social origin, property, birth or other status. Furthermore, no

distinction shall be made on the basis of the political, jurisdictional or international status of the country or territory to which a

person belongs, whether it be independent, trust, non-self-governing or under any other limitation of sovereignty.


Parts of the Declaration refer to the rights to quality of life, which would be affected by the ability to access clean water.


Article 5

No one shall be subjected to torture or to cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment.


While the pollution of water is not necessarily torture, nor has as significant of an effect on people. It is cruel to those who

drink and/or live near or in it. It is not necessary nor beneficial.


Article 7

All are equal before the law and are entitled without any discrimination to equal protection of the law. All are entitled to equal

protection against any discrimination in violation of this Declaration and against any incitement to such discrimination.


All people should have the shared right to safe water, to give one community safe water, and the other dirty water, the state is

not providing equal to protection to both communities.

Philosipher

John Locke once argued that God had "given the world to man in common," and thus wanted man to make use of it best. Every man was

given the resources and had equal right to it, yet ownership rights were shifted once somebody altered or developed that resource.

In the case of large oil companies, one would imagine that by Locke's logic, the detrimental effects on water would be "owned" by

those companies, and they would feel the disadvantage of their development. However, those working in large oil companies are not the

ones negatively affected by the water, and in fact often benefit from these conditions. Therefore, the actions of these companies are

unequal and disrupt the responsibilities of resource ownership.

Catholic Social Teaching

Catholic tradition insists that we show our respect for the Creator by our stewardship of creation. Care for the earth is not just

an Earth Day slogan, it is a requirement of our faith. We are called to protect people and the planet, living our faith in relationship

with all of God's creation. This environmental challenge has fundamental moral and ethical dimensions that cannot be ignored.


This Catholic Social Teaching states that me must be stewards of the earth. Water is the majority of the earth and is a vital element

to all forms of life. As Stewards of the earth we must take care of water, and activly work to not pollute and if not, then clean up water

pollution left behind by others.
